Software



Premiere Pro

For me, this is the best video editing software overall (sorry, finalcut users)
It’s available for both Mac and PC users and it offers everything you need for professional video editing.
I love Premiere Pro’s Lumetri Color tool. It offers color adjustment and manipulation almost close to Photoshop.
It's also flexible, and an easy-to-use interface. I use Premiere Pro in editing some of my baby’s videos,
wedding highlights, and other video documentaries.

FinalCut Pro

This editing software runs on Mac personal computers only. So if you are not a Mac user, skip this part and wait for us on the next bullet.
A lot of famous movies have been edited with this program, such as Social Network, Cold Mountain and many others.
It is widely known and is very popular among filmmakers. It has a wide range of functionality and is big enough to handle motion pictures.

Adobe Premiere Elements

Are you new to video editing? Then Premiere Elements is your best option.
It's a simplified version of Premiere Pro and it's a great place to get started.

Da Vinci Resolve

This software is simple but enough. If you previously used Premiere,
you can can import some of your keyboard shortcuts from Premiere to Da Vinci so you won’t have to set it again.

What I love about Da Vinci is- it offers universal app support to provide better performance on small laptops
and it's a powerful tool for color correcting. It's only downside is- it is a little confusing for newbies.

LumaFusion

No laptop, no pc, no mac? No worries, you can start your editing career on your phone!
LumaFusion is a mobile editing app for iPhones (ok, skip this if you’re an Android user or think about shifting)

LumaFusion is a one-time-payment app. That means there’s no monthly or annual subscription, just pay $30 and it's yours for life!
I use LumaFusion for my same day edit videos (SDEs) because it is convenient.

You can edit anywhere while having the same quality as PC software editors like Adobe Premiere.
Haters would say video quality drops when you use a mobile app
(try telling that to apple, LOL) but don’t believe them, it’s the same quality I tell you.

You can render 720p to 4k file outputs, perform some color corrections,
or import look-up tables (LUTs) in LumaFusion, finally rendering is really fast.
